# Deadwater image direction guide

Use this for homepage images, hero images, section backplates, blog visuals, and future generated art direction when the goal is to match the current site.

## Core image mood

- submerged
- engineered
- cinematic
- moody
- sharp but not sterile
- slightly mysterious

Images should feel like infrastructure below the surface, not cheerful stock marketing.

## Preferred image traits

- dark overall value range
- strong contrast with selective highlights
- blue, teal, slate, steel, black
- water, fog, haze, glow, depth, reflections
- industrial or architectural forms
- minimal scenes with one dominant focal structure
- premium editorial framing

## Composition rules

- Favor wide cinematic crops.
- Leave room for text overlays where needed.
- Use one strong subject, not many competing objects.
- Strong silhouettes and horizon lines work well.
- Negative space is useful. Do not fill every inch of the frame.

## Texture and finish

- Slight haze or atmosphere is good.
- Reflections, mist, and soft bloom are good.
- Avoid crunchy over-sharpening.
- Avoid flat vector illustrations unless the page already uses an illustrative system.

## Subject matter that fits

- submerged architecture
- underwater structures
- industrial forms disappearing into darkness
- layered maps, diagrams, or abstract systems visuals
- oceanic or liminal environments with engineered elements

## Subject matter to avoid

- smiling office teams
- generic laptops on desks
- obvious AI robot art
- cartoon brains, glowing circuit heads, or prompt-window cliches
- bright flat stock photos
- busy collages with too many focal points

## Color rules

- Stay in the ink / cyan / sea family.
- Use warm tones only as minor contrast, not the base palette.
- Do not introduce loud pink, purple, orange, or lime as primary image colors.

## Relationship to page design

- Images should support the glass-panel UI, not fight it.
- The page chrome is already visually rich, so images should be restrained and atmospheric.
- Use images as depth anchors, not as loud attention traps.

## When generating new images

Prompt for:

- cinematic underwater or near-water atmosphere
- engineered structure or abstract system geometry
- dark editorial lighting
- premium tech brand feel
- room for typography
- restrained cyan/teal highlights

Avoid prompting for:

- futuristic city overload
- comic-book sci-fi
- glossy 3D startup blobs
- humanoid robots
- busy dashboard screenshots passed off as hero art

## Simple reference sentence

If the image would feel at home behind a premium deep-sea control room interface, it probably fits Deadwater.
